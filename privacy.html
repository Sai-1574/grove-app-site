  • Use Treeak. Treeak is a calm, beautifully made habit tracker for people who want to build a life of small consistent actions, not chase another streak counter. Every habit you tend feeds a living tree at the heart of the app. Tap your habits done each day and the tree stays thriving. Miss too many in a row and it slowly fades through five vitality stages, from Thriving through Fading, Wilting, Deteriorating, and finally Dormant. The tree is not a gimmick. It is a quiet, honest mirror of how the past few weeks have actually gone, recovering as your consistency comes back.

    What you can track. Track daily habits, weekly goals (do something N times a week), and count-based habits (read 10 pages, do 50 push-ups). Reorder with a long-press. Pause individual habits or take a full vacation. Insure a missed day with a planned skip. Add notes against any past day in the habit's calendar timeline.

    A living journal. Capture your mood, write a few lines, attach a photo or a voice note. Treeak keeps every entry private on your device and never reads it.

    Insight that respects your time. A clean Insights tab with consistency heatmaps, streak history, per-habit progress, peak hours, best days, monthly trends, and weekly reports for Premium users. Numbers when you want them, never in your face.

    Privacy at the centre. Treeak runs entirely on your device. No accounts, no servers, no analytics, no advertising trackers, no third-party data sharing. The only optional connection is a backup to your own Google Drive, into the hidden app folder that even the developer cannot see.

    Treeak Premium. Unlock weekly tree reports, deep analytics, the full theme library, journal photos and voice notes, the consistency heatmap, and Google Drive backup. Monthly, yearly, and lifetime plans available.

9. Controls for do-not-track features

Most web browsers and some mobile operating systems and mobile applications include a Do-Not-Track ('DNT') feature or setting you can activate to signal your privacy preference not to have data about your online browsing activities monitored and collected. At this stage, no uniform technology standard for recognising and implementing DNT signals has been finalised. As such, we do not currently respond to DNT browser signals or any other mechanism that automatically communicates your choice not to be tracked online. If a standard for online tracking is adopted that we must follow in the future, we will inform you about that practice in a revised version of this Privacy Notice.

California law requires us to let you know how we respond to web browser DNT signals. Because there currently is not an industry or legal standard for recognising or honouring DNT signals, we do not respond to them at this time.

13. Local-first architecture and no server

Local-first architecture. Treeak is built as a local-first application. There is no Treeak-operated server that stores, collects, or has access to your habits, journal entries, mood ratings, or any other content you create in the app. All such data resides on your device. The only network communication initiated by the app is to Google's servers (for the optional Drive backup you control), to Apple's or Google's app store servers (for subscription transactions), and to RevenueCat (for purchase entitlement verification).
