Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 19, 2026 · Developer contact: saiveshwa15@gmail.com

What Treeak is

Treeak is a habit-tracking app with a journaling feature. It is provided by an independent developer and is not affiliated with Google.

What data Treeak collects and where it is stored

1. Local-only by default. Everything you enter in Treeak — habits, completions, journal entries (text, mood, photos, voice notes), weekly reports, themes, your name, and focus goal — is stored locally on your device. Treeak does not run a server, does not have user accounts, and does not transmit your data to any third party except as described below.

2. Optional Google Drive backup. If you tap Connect Google Drive in Settings, Treeak asks for permission to use your Google account's hidden application data folder (the drive.appdata scope). When granted, Treeak uploads a JSON snapshot of your habits, journal entries, weekly reports, and settings to that folder, along with any photos and voice notes attached to journal entries. The drive.appdata scope is restricted by Google so Treeak can only read and write files it created in this hidden folder — Treeak cannot see, list, or modify any of your other Google Drive files.

3. Email address. When you connect Google Drive, Treeak reads your account's email address (via the userinfo.email and openid scopes) solely to display it in the Settings screen so you can confirm which account is connected. The email is stored locally on the device and is not transmitted anywhere outside Google's own servers.

What Treeak does NOT collect

  • Treeak does not use analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or tracking SDKs.
  • Treeak does not sell, share, or rent any user data.
  • Treeak does not request access to contacts, calendar, location, SMS, call logs, or any sensitive system data.

How long data is kept

Local data remains on your device until you delete the app or clear its storage. Cloud snapshots remain in your Google Drive's application data folder until you delete them. Treeak retains the most recent five snapshots and removes older ones automatically.

How to delete your data

  • Local data: uninstall Treeak, or clear the app's storage in Android Settings → Apps → Treeak → Storage → Clear data.
  • Cloud backup: tap Disconnect Google Drive in Treeak's Settings to revoke Treeak's access. To permanently delete the backup files themselves, visit myaccount.google.com/permissions, select Treeak, and remove access — Google will then prompt you to delete Treeak's hidden app data, or you can do so from drive.google.com → Settings → Manage apps.

Children

Treeak is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from them.

Third-party services

Google Drive is the only third-party service Treeak communicates with, and only when you have explicitly connected your account. Google's handling of the data it receives is governed by Google's own privacy policy: policies.google.com/privacy.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new effective date. Continued use of Treeak after changes constitutes acceptance.
