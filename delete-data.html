Delete Your Data

Last updated May 27, 2026

Treeak is a local-first habit tracker. Most of your data never leaves your device. This page explains the three places your data can live and how to delete each one.

If you cannot complete the steps below yourself (for example, you no longer have access to the device), you can email the developer at saiveshwa15@gmail.com with a deletion request. Acknowledgement within 7 days; resolution within 30 days.

1. Data on your device (always present)

Every habit, completion tap, journal entry, photo, voice note, mood rating, and theme preference is stored locally on your phone using Android's app-private storage. None of it is sent to any server we control.

Option A — Clear in-app (recommended if you still want to keep the app installed)

  1. Open Treeak.
  2. Go to Settings (the gear tab at the far right of the bottom bar).
  3. If you have connected Google Drive, scroll to the Sync section and tap Disconnect. Choose "Disconnect and clear my data." This wipes every habit, journal entry, photo, and voice note on the device.
  4. If you have not connected Drive, your only on-device clear option is to uninstall (Option B) or use Android's app storage controls (Option C).

Option B — Uninstall the app

Uninstalling Treeak from your phone deletes every byte of local data the app holds. Long-press the Treeak icon on your home screen and choose Uninstall, or go to Android Settings › Apps › Treeak › Uninstall.

Option C — Clear app storage from Android settings

If you want to wipe data without uninstalling: Android Settings › Apps › Treeak › Storage & cache › Clear storage. This is equivalent to a fresh install — all habits, journal entries, and preferences are removed.

2. Data in your Google Drive (only if you connected Drive)

If you connected Google Drive for backup, Treeak stores encrypted snapshots and journal media in a hidden, app-private folder on your own Drive. You can read this folder only with Treeak; it is not visible in the regular Drive UI. The data has never left your Google account.

Option A — Delete from inside the app

The fastest way:

  1. Open Treeak › Settings › Sync.
  2. Tap Disconnect and choose "Disconnect and clear my data."
  3. This severs the connection on this device. To also remove the hidden Drive folder, continue with Option B.

Option B — Remove Treeak's hidden Drive data directly

  1. Visit drive.google.com in a browser and sign in.
  2. Click the gear icon in the top right and choose Settings.
  3. In the left sidebar, choose Manage apps.
  4. Find Treeak in the list of connected apps.
  5. Click Options › Delete hidden app data. Confirm.

This permanently deletes every backup snapshot and journal photo or voice note Treeak ever wrote to your Drive. The Drive deletion is irreversible.

Option C — Revoke the authorization entirely

  1. Visit myaccount.google.com/permissions.
  2. Find Treeak and click Remove access.

This invalidates Treeak's refresh token so the app can no longer reach your Drive. Combine with Option B to also delete the hidden folder.

3. Data held by RevenueCat (purchases only)

When you buy Treeak Premium on Google Play, our payments partner RevenueCat receives an anonymized purchase token from the Play Store so it can verify your subscription is active. They do not receive your email, name, or any in-app content. The anonymized identifier they assign you takes the form $RCAnonymousID:<random>.

RevenueCat retains a minimal purchase record for finance-and-refund processing for the period required by their data retention policy. To request deletion of your RevenueCat record, email saiveshwa15@gmail.com with the subject line "RevenueCat record deletion" and your Google Play order ID (visible in Play Store › Payments & subscriptions › Budget & history). Acknowledgement within 7 days; resolution within 30 days.

What is kept after deletion

Once you complete the steps above, nothing of yours remains on your device. The hidden Drive folder is deleted at Google's normal pace once you trigger "Delete hidden app data."

Treeak does not maintain server-side backups, analytics, telemetry, or any other copy of your data. There is nothing else for the developer to delete on your behalf, except the RevenueCat purchase record described in Section 3 above.
